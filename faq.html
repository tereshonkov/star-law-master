Frequently Asked Questions

Below we answer common questions about payment disputes, online brokers, trading platforms and crypto-related issues. The information is general only and does not replace tailored legal advice.

1. What types of disputes do you handle?

We focus on legal issues connected with electronic payments and online financial services, including:

  • Disputes over card payments, chargebacks and reversed transactions.
  • Conflicts with online brokers and investment platforms, such as blocked balances or rejected withdrawals.
  • Cases involving payment service providers, acquiring banks and merchant accounts.
  • Matters related to crypto exchanges, custodial wallets and digital-asset payment services.

Each inquiry is assessed individually, and we may not be able to assist in every situation.

2. Can you guarantee that my money will be returned?

No. We do not guarantee recovery of any funds or a particular outcome in any dispute. The result of a matter depends on:

  • The specific facts and timeline of your case.
  • The contracts and terms you accepted with banks, brokers or platforms.
  • The quality and completeness of supporting evidence.
  • The applicable law and regulatory environment.
  • The responses and decisions of counterparties and authorities.

Our role is to provide legal analysis and representation within the boundaries of applicable law and professional rules, not to promise a particular financial result.

3. Do you provide investment or trading advice?

We do not provide investment, trading or financial advice. We do not manage trading accounts, select financial products or recommend when to enter or exit positions.

Our work is centred on legal aspects: contracts, procedures, regulatory obligations, communication with institutions and dispute resolution mechanisms.

4. How does the initial legal consultation work?

If, after a preliminary review of your inquiry, we consider that your matter may fall within our practice areas, we may offer an initial legal consultation. During this consultation we typically:

  • Clarify key facts (who, what, when, where).
  • Review essential documents at a high level, if available.
  • Discuss possible legal options and limitations.
  • Explain the next steps if both sides are interested in moving forward.

The consultation itself does not guarantee that we will accept the case or that any particular outcome will be achieved.

5. What information should I prepare before contacting you?

To help us understand your situation more efficiently, it is useful to have:

  • The names of banks, brokers, platforms or payment services involved.
  • Key dates (payments, withdrawals, account blocks, correspondence).
  • Amounts at issue and currency.
  • Screenshots or statements showing relevant transactions.
  • Copies or links to terms and conditions, agreements or contracts.

Please avoid sending full document collections or sensitive information until we confirm whether we can review your matter and explain the secure way to share materials.

6. Can you help with disputes involving crypto exchanges and wallets?

In many cases we can examine disputes connected with crypto exchanges, custodial wallets and related payment services, especially where there are issues with withdrawals, frozen accounts, KYC/AML checks or interpretation of platform terms.

Our analysis focuses on legal aspects — contractual documentation, communication history and applicable regulatory framework — not on providing technical blockchain support or trading strategies.

7. Are your services available in my country?

The availability of our services depends on jurisdiction, local regulations and professional rules. In some matters we may cooperate with other legal professionals or local counsel where appropriate.

When you contact us, please indicate your country of residence and where the bank, broker or platform is located. This helps us quickly understand whether we may be able to assist.

8. How are your fees determined?

Our fees are determined based on factors such as:

  • The complexity and estimated duration of the matter.
  • The volume of documents and analysis required.
  • The jurisdictions and applicable law involved.
  • The type of work (advice, correspondence, negotiation, representation).

Before any engagement is confirmed, we provide information about the proposed fee structure in accordance with professional and regulatory requirements. Nothing on this website constitutes an offer, quote or binding fee agreement.

9. Does sending a contact form create an attorney–client relationship?

No. Simply sending us a contact form, email or message does not create an attorney–client relationship. Such a relationship may be formed only after:

  • We have performed necessary conflict checks.
  • We have confirmed that we are able to act in your matter under applicable rules.
  • Both you and we have explicitly agreed to the engagement, usually in writing.

10. What should I do if I suspect fraud or a scam?

If you suspect that you have been affected by fraud or a scam, consider taking the following general steps:

  • Collect and safely store all relevant evidence (emails, screenshots, statements).
  • Contact your bank or payment provider promptly to report the issue.
  • Consider reporting the matter to the competent authorities in your jurisdiction.

We may, subject to conflicts and professional rules, be able to review your situation from a legal perspective. However, we cannot unilaterally reverse transactions, stop ongoing schemes or guarantee that any authority or institution will take a particular action.

11. How long does a payment or broker dispute usually take?

Timeframes vary widely. Some payment disputes or chargebacks may be resolved within a few weeks, while complex broker or platform disputes can take many months or longer.

Duration depends on the institutions involved, regulatory procedures, the availability of evidence and the legal strategy chosen. No fixed timeline can be promised in advance.

12. Can you communicate directly with my bank or broker?

Where permitted and once an engagement is in place, we may correspond with banks, brokers or payment providers on your behalf as part of the legal work.

However, these institutions remain independent and make their own decisions under internal policies and applicable law. We cannot force them to accept a particular position or outcome.

13. Can you help if I already tried a chargeback or complaint on my own?

In some cases we can review matters where you have already submitted a chargeback, complaint or claim. Previous correspondence and decisions will be important for our assessment.

A rejected complaint does not automatically mean that no options remain, but it also does not guarantee that further steps will succeed. We provide a realistic view based on the available material.

14. Do you work with cases involving unregulated or offshore brokers?

We may review disputes involving unregulated or offshore brokers, but available legal tools can be more limited compared with fully regulated entities.

Enforcement and recovery options in such situations may be constrained, and expectations must remain realistic. No recovery can be promised solely because a broker acted improperly.

15. Can you assist with regulatory complaints or reports to authorities?

Subject to jurisdiction and professional rules, we may assist in drafting or structuring complaints to financial regulators or other competent authorities.

Regulators are independent bodies and we cannot influence or guarantee their decisions or the timing of their procedures.

16. Can I use this website instead of consulting a lawyer in my country?

No. Content on this website is general information only. It cannot replace personalised advice from a qualified lawyer who is authorised to practise in your jurisdiction and familiar with your full situation.

Decisions about legal action should not be based solely on website materials.

17. Do you cooperate with other law firms or local counsel?

Where appropriate, and subject to conflicts and confidentiality requirements, we may cooperate with other law firms or local counsel to address cross-border or local aspects of a matter.

Any such cooperation is structured in line with professional and regulatory rules of the jurisdictions involved.
