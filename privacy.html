PRIVACY POLICY

HOW WE HANDLE YOUR DATA
WHEN YOU USE OUR WEBSITE AND CONTACT FORMS

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Transaction Helpdesk Ltd (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses and protects personal information when you visit this website or contact us using the forms or contact details provided here. The information below is for general purposes and does not create an attorney–client relationship.

1. Information we may collect

We may collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Identification and contact details such as your name, email address, phone number and country or region.
  • Case-related information that you choose to provide in your message or attached documents, including platform names, dates, transaction details and basic description of your dispute.
  • Technical data such as IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited and time spent on the website, collected through standard web server logs and similar technologies.

2. How we use your information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To review your inquiry and contact you about a potential initial consultation.
  • To assess, at a general level, whether your matter may fall within our practice areas.
  • To operate, maintain and improve this website and its security.
  • To comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations.

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third-party marketing companies.

3. Legal basis for processing

Depending on your location and applicable law, our legal basis for processing personal data may include:

  • Your consent when you voluntarily submit information through our contact form or by email.
  • Our legitimate interest in responding to inquiries, operating our practice and managing potential or existing client relationships.
  • Legal obligations where we are required to keep certain records or cooperate with competent authorities.

4. How we share information

We may share your information, where appropriate and subject to confidentiality obligations, with:

  • Our lawyers and staff involved in reviewing your matter.
  • IT and hosting service providers who support operation of this website and our communication systems.
  • Other legal professionals or local counsel in different jurisdictions, when necessary to assess your case and only with appropriate safeguards.
  • Public authorities, regulators or courts, where we are legally required to do so.

Any such sharing is limited to what is necessary for the relevant purpose and is carried out in line with applicable law.

5. Data retention

We retain personal data for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, including responding to your inquiry, complying with legal and regulatory requirements and managing potential or existing legal matters. The specific retention period may vary depending on the nature of your request and applicable legal rules.

6. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, alteration or disclosure. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights in relation to your personal data, such as:

  • The right to request access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • The right to request deletion of your data, subject to legal or professional obligations.
  • The right to object to or request restriction of certain types of processing.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details provided in the “Contact” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding to your request.

8. Cookies and similar technologies

This website may use cookies or similar technologies to improve functionality, analyse basic usage statistics and enhance user experience. You can usually control cookies through your browser settings, including blocking or deleting them. Some parts of the site may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

9. Third-party websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, platforms or services. We are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of those third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any external sites you visit.

10. Children’s data

Our services and this website are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so that we can take appropriate steps.

11. Updates to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements or website features. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised “Last updated” date. We encourage you to review this Policy periodically.
